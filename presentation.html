Lunar Rails operates across different branches of the Bitcoin ecosystem. Mining adds an infrastructure layer to that presence. Evaluating deals across multiple geographies requires a purpose-built tool.
WHY WE MOVED BEYOND EXCEL
1
Version Control
No single source of truth. Hard to know if everyone had the latest version of the model.
2
Inefficient Updates
Changing a core variable like the start date required manual updates across dozens of cells and sheets.
3
Basic Market Assumptions
BTC price and hashrate projections were flat or simple linear.
4
No Live Hardware Data
Miner specs and pricing were manually researched and quickly outdated. No connection to manufacturer or broker data.
5
No Deal Comparison
Each deal lived in its own file. Comparing two opportunities side by side meant copying numbers into yet another spreadsheet.
HOW BTC MINING REVENUE WORKS

Mining machines (ASICs) produce computing power called hashpower, measured in terahashes per second (TH/s). The more hashpower you deploy, the more Bitcoin you earn.

BTC Price
Directly multiplies your revenue. A 2x price means 2x revenue.
Network Hashrate
The more miners join, the smaller your slice. You compete for a fixed reward pool.
These two variables are external and uncertain, which is why the dashboard models them across multiple scenarios.
THE DASHBOARD
All four pages share the same data. Change a miner selection or scenario assumption, and the dashboard recalculates instantly.
